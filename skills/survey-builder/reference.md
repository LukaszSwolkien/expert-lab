# Survey Builder — Question Bank & Examples

This reference contains reusable question patterns organized by category. These are starting points — always tailor to the specific situation. Do not include all questions from a category; select the ones that address the user's concern.

## Likert Scale (used for all scaled questions)

| Score | Label |
|---|---|
| 1 | Strongly disagree |
| 2 | Disagree |
| 3 | Neutral |
| 4 | Agree |
| 5 | Strongly agree |

## Category: Delivery & Ownership

| Question | Measures |
|---|---|
| When this person takes ownership of a task, I am confident it will be completed to the team's agreed standards and brought to DONE within a reasonable timeframe. | Delivery reliability, ownership, execution quality |
| When this person commits to a deadline, update, or follow-up, I can rely on it without needing reminders. | Accountability, dependability, commitment integrity |
| This person makes decisions that prioritize team and PI goals (e.g., focusing on highest-impact work before starting new tasks). | Prioritization, goal orientation, business alignment |
| If I had a critical, time-sensitive task, I would trust this person to handle it. | Trust under pressure, reliability in high-stakes situations |

## Category: Collaboration & Team Alignment

| Question | Measures |
|---|---|
| This person follows team agreements (e.g., working norms, tools, processes, code review expectations), and openly raises concerns when they disagree. | Team alignment, process discipline, constructive transparency |
| This person responds effectively to code review feedback (addresses comments or clearly explains trade-offs when not applying a suggestion). | Collaboration quality, engineering communication, feedback responsiveness |
| This person asks for help at the right time and in the right amount. | Self-awareness, collaboration balance, delivery judgment |
| This person is able to discuss, agree, and negotiate terms of technical discussions — respecting team decisions and ceding when appropriate. | Technical discussion quality, negotiation, team decision respect |

## Category: Communication & Professionalism

| Question | Measures |
|---|---|
| This person communicates effectively with team members (information transfer is smooth, clear, accurate, and without noise). | Communication clarity, signal-to-noise ratio |
| This person demonstrates adequate professional behaviors with partners — including respect, professionalism, and willingness to disagree constructively, even in hard moments. | Professional behavior, respectful disagreement, composure |
| This person proactively raises risks or issues that could impact team performance or morale (e.g., role confusion, unclear requirements, technical direction, team behaviors). | Proactive communication, risk awareness, team health |

## Category: Growth & Team Impact

| Question | Measures |
|---|---|
| This person is open to sharing knowledge and innovative ideas within the team and across other development teams. | Knowledge sharing, cross-team collaboration, innovation mindset |
| Working with this person is motivating. | Team energy, positive influence, collaboration experience |
| I learn a lot from working with this person. | Peer development impact, mentorship, learning contribution |
| This person consistently demonstrates commitment to team goals and is willing to take on challenges. | Team commitment, growth mindset, challenge readiness |

## Category: Safety & Trust (diagnostic)

| Question | Measures |
|---|---|
| I feel comfortable raising concerns about technical decisions in this team. | Psychological safety, openness to dissent |
| When I disagree with this person, I feel heard and respected. | Interpersonal trust, receptiveness to feedback |
| I trust this person's judgment in areas where I have less expertise. | Cross-domain trust, competence-based trust |

## Open-Ended Question Patterns

### Pattern A: Improvement-focused (forward-looking)
- What could this person do differently to improve collaboration with others?
- What is one thing this person could change that would have the biggest positive impact on the team?

### Pattern B: Diagnostic (current state)
- What is this person doing well that they should keep doing?
- What is not working well in how you collaborate with this person?

### Pattern C: Broad discovery
- What is OK?
- What is not OK?
- What am I missing?

### Pattern D: Self-improvement (for self-initiated surveys)
- What am I doing well?
- What can I improve?
- How can I be more effective at my job?
- What do I need to learn to be more effective?

## Survey Segmentation Examples

### 360 with internal/external split

Send the same scaled questions to two groups:
- **Internal group** — team members who work with the person daily (same squad/team).
- **External group** — cross-team collaborators, product managers, stakeholders.

Compare average scores per question between groups. A gap of 0.8+ points on the same question signals a perception difference worth investigating.

### Function-based segmentation

Tag respondents by function (engineering, product, design, QA, ops) without revealing individual identity. Useful when:
- The person interacts very differently with each function.
- Cross-functional friction is the suspected issue.

Minimum 4-5 respondents per segment to maintain anonymity.

## Interpretation Quick Reference

| Signal | What it means | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Average score ≤ 2.5 on any question (8+ respondents) | Strong negative signal | Investigate — this is not noise |
| Score variance > 1.5 on a question | Polarized perception | Some people see something others don't — dig into segments |
| Safety question scores ≤ 3.0 | People may not be answering honestly | Consider follow-up 1:1s or a different feedback mechanism |
| Internal vs. external gap > 0.8 points | Different perception inside vs. outside team | The person may behave differently by audience — or the audiences have different expectations |
| 3+ respondents mention the same theme in open-ended | Pattern, not anecdote | Strong enough to discuss directly with the person |
| Open-ended answers are very short or empty | Survey fatigue or fear of attribution | Shorten the survey next time; reinforce anonymity |
